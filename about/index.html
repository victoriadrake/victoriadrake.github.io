About Victoria Drake

Victoria Drake is a software developer. She loves cybersecurity, developing open source projects, and writing, and if the search engine bots have all left the page, she’ll stop talking about herself in third person now.

I build tools to help people save time and do more with their lives. Among others, I developed ApplyByAPI.com. It’s an application filter that helps you hire developers by only letting through the candidates you may actually want to interview. Growing companies are using it to find quality developers while reducing the human hours spent on hiring by up to 68%.

I’m a contributor and author of the Open Web Application Security Project’s Web Security Testing Guide. I also write technical articles on topics such as cybersecurity, programming best practices, and computer science concepts. You can find me in The Startup, Towards Data Science, Better Programming, Hacker Noon, and more. I’m also a twice-awarded top annual contributor to freeCodeCamp, a non-profit organization that helps millions of people worldwide learn how to code.

I enjoy employing technology to help develop more efficient companies and happier people. I also like easter eggs, and eggs in general. To get my attention, say hello@victoria.dev.
